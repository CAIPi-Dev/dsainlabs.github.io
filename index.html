The Problem

The same pattern. Three domains. One company.

Confidence without evidence is the most dangerous form of misinformation — because it's the hardest to detect. We call this the Augmentation-Reliability Paradox.

🏛

In the Boardroom

AI generates strategic recommendations that sound authoritative but can't trace reasoning to verified evidence. Boards can't audit what they can't see. No governance framework exists for AI-augmented decisions.

⚖

In Regulated Industries

The EU AI Act requires independent assessment of high-risk AI systems by August 2026. LLM providers cannot credibly self-certify — the same way accounting firms cannot audit their own books. A structural gap exists.

👤

In Everyday Life

AI chatbots reinforce delusions. Headlines manipulate emotions. Phishing exploits psychological pressure. The confidence-without-evidence pattern operates everywhere — and nobody was making it visible.

Enterprise

Independent LLM Reliability Assessment

The same auditor independence principle that prevents accounting firms from auditing their own books creates structural demand for third-party AI output verification. DSAIN fills that gap.

  • Evidence Grounding APIVerify LLM claims against authoritative domain-specific databases — financial, legal, medical, scientific.

Research Foundation

This isn't a weekend project. It's a multi-year research program.

DSAIN is built on doctoral research into the governance of AI-augmented strategic decision-making. The theoretical framework, the experimental design, and the working system are inseparable — the artifact embodies the theory.

The Augmentation-Reliability Paradox (ARP) posits that AI systems that most effectively relax bounded rationality simultaneously create conditions where bounded reliability failures are most consequential. The same features that make AI articulate and capable make it prone to fabrication and sycophancy.
